Touring the nation's capital
12 best places to tour in Washington, DC
4.2
The White House
Free Admission.
1600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, Washington, D.C.
The White House is the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States, located at 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW in Washington, D.C. It has been the residence of every U.S. president since John Adams in 1800.
Official site
4.2
The United States Capitol
Free Admission.
East Capitol St NE & First St SE, Washington, DC 20004
The United States Capitol, often called the Capitol Building, is the home of the United States Congress, and the seat of the legislative branch of the U.S. federal government.
Official Site
4.7
The Lincoln Memorial
Free Admission
2 Lincoln Memorial Cir NW, Washington, DC 20037
The Lincoln Memorial is an American national monument built to honor the 16th President of the United States, Abraham Lincoln. It is located on the western end of the National Mall in Washington, D.C., across from the Washington Monument.
Official site
4.5
The National Monument
Free Admission
2 15th St NW, Washington, DC 20024
The Washington Monument is an obelisk on the National Mall in Washington, D.C., built to commemorate George Washington, once commander-in-chief of the Continental Army and the first President of the United States.
Official site
4.3
Smithsonian National Museum of American History
Free Admission
14th St and Constitution Ave, NW, Washington, DC 20001
The National Museum of American History: Kenneth E. Behring Center collects, preserves, and displays the heritage of the United States in the areas of social, political, cultural, scientific, and military history.
Official site
4.3
National Zoological Park
Free Admission
Open from 08:00 - 17h00
3001 Connecticut Ave NW, Washington, DC 20008
The National Zoological Park, commonly known as the National Zoo, is one of the oldest zoos in the United States. It is part of the Smithsonian Institution and does not charge for admission.
Official Site
4.7
The Jefferson Memorial
Free Admission
701 E Basin Dr SW, Washington, DC 20242
The Jefferson Memorial is a presidential memorial in Washington, D.C., dedicated to Thomas Jefferson (1743–1826), one of the most important of the American Founding Fathers as the main drafter and writer of the Declaration of Independence.
Official Site
4.1
International Spy Museum
Children 6 & under(FREE)
7 & up(start from $14.95)
800 F St NW, Washington, DC 20004
The International Spy Museum is a privately owned museum dedicated to the tradecraft, history and contemporary role of espionage, featuring the largest collection of international espionage artifacts currently on public display.
Official Site
4.5
Newseum
Open from 09:00 - 17h00
555 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20001
The Newseum is an interactive museum that promotes free expression and the First Amendment to the United States Constitution, while tracing the evolution of communication.
Official site
4.7
National Museum of African American History and Culture
Need Time passes
1400 Constitution Ave NW, Washington, DC 20560
The National Museum of African American History and Culture is a Smithsonian Institution museum established in December 2003. The museum's building, designed by David Adjaye, is on the National Mall in Washington, D.C..
Official site
4.6
National Air and Space Museum
600 Independence Ave SW, Washington, DC 20560
The National Air and Space Museum of the Smithsonian Institution, also called the NASM, is a museum in Washington, D.C.. It holds the largest collection of historic aircraft and spacecraft in the world.
Official Site
4.6
National Gallery of Art
Open from 10:00 - 17h00
6th & Constitution Ave NW, Washington, DC 20565
The National Gallery of Art, and its attached Sculpture Garden, is a national art museum in Washington, D.C., located on the National Mall, between 3rd and 9th Streets, at Constitution Avenue NW
Official site
